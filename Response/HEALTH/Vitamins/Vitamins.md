# 1. What Are Vitamins?

Vitamins are **organic compounds** required by the body in **small amounts** for normal functioning.

They help in:

- Growth
- Immunity
- Energy metabolism
- Nerve function
- Blood formation
- Vision
- Bone health
- Healing

## Important:
Vitamins do **not** provide calories like:

- Carbohydrates
- Proteins
- Fats

They act as **regulators**.

# 2. Types of Vitamins

## A. Fat-Soluble Vitamins

Stored in body fat and liver.

Can accumulate.

## Mnemonic:
**ADEK**

- Vitamin A
- Vitamin D
- Vitamin E
- Vitamin K

---

## B. Water-Soluble Vitamins

Not stored much in body  
(except B12 to some extent)

Need regular intake.

Includes:

- Vitamin C
- B-complex vitamins

# 3. Fat-Soluble Vitamins

---

# Vitamin A (Retinol)

## Functions
- Vision (especially night vision)
- Skin health
- Immunity
- Cell growth

## Deficiency
- Night blindness
- Dry eyes
- Poor immunity

## Sources
- Carrot
- Sweet potato
- Spinach
- Egg yolk
- Liver

---

# Vitamin D

## Main Function
Helps absorb calcium.

Formula idea:

```text
Vitamin D → Increased Calcium Absorption
```

## Roles
- Strong bones
- Teeth health
- Immune support

## Deficiency
- Rickets (children)
- Osteomalacia
- Weak bones

## Sources
- Sunlight
- Fish
- Egg yolk
- Fortified milk

---

# Vitamin E

## Functions
- Antioxidant
- Protects cells

## Sources
- Nuts
- Seeds
- Almonds
- Vegetable oils

## Deficiency (rare)
- Nerve issues
- Muscle weakness

---

# Vitamin K

## Function
Blood clotting

```text
Vitamin K → Clotting Factors
```

## Sources
- Green leafy vegetables

## Deficiency
- Excess bleeding

# 5. B-Complex Vitamins

Think:

```text
Energy + Nerves
```

---

## Vitamin B1 — Thiamine

### Function
- Energy metabolism
- Nerve function

### Deficiency
- Beriberi

### Sources
- Whole grains
- Legumes

---

## Vitamin B2 — Riboflavin

### Function
- Energy production

### Deficiency
- Cracked lips
- Mouth sores

---

## Vitamin B3 — Niacin

### Function
- Metabolism

### Deficiency
Pellagra

## 3 Ds of Pellagra
- Dermatitis
- Diarrhea
- Dementia

---

## Vitamin B5 — Pantothenic Acid

### Function
- Helps make coenzymes

---

## Vitamin B6 — Pyridoxine

### Functions
- Protein metabolism
- Neurotransmitters

### Deficiency
- Nerve symptoms
- Anemia

---

## Vitamin B7 — Biotin

Associated with:

- Hair
- Skin
- Nails

---

## Vitamin B9 — Folate

### Important for
- DNA synthesis
- Red blood cell formation

### Deficiency
- Megaloblastic anemia

## Special Note
Very important in pregnancy.

---

## Vitamin B12 — Cobalamin

Very important vitamin.

## Functions
- Nerve function
- Red blood cell formation
- DNA synthesis

## Deficiency
- Anemia
- Neuropathy

## Sources
Mostly animal foods:

- Milk
- Eggs
- Meat

- Carbohydrates = Fuel
