## / Overview

**ThinkSphere** is a curated intellectual community built around one premise: most online discourse is fast, shallow, and forgettable. This space is designed to be the opposite.

We engage with podcasts, lectures, essays, and primary sources — then write about them with clarity, honesty, and depth. Every contribution here is a record of a mind actively working through something difficult.

This is not a feed. It is a practice.

---

## / Why This Exists

The internet has made information infinitely accessible and reflection increasingly rare. ThinkSphere exists to close that gap — to build a space where:

- Thinking is treated as a **discipline**, not a pastime
- Writing is the **output** of understanding, not a substitute for it
- Diverse viewpoints are engaged **seriously**, not just tolerated
- Intellectual discomfort is treated as **signal**, not noise

## / How to Contribute

```bash
# Step 1 — Select a source
  Choose a podcast episode, TED Talk, lecture, paper, or essay.

# Step 2 — Engage with it
  Watch, listen, or read. Take notes. Sit with it.

# Step 3 — Write your POV
  Construct an original response. Aim for 300–800 words.
  Challenge the ideas. Build on them. Disagree precisely.

# Step 4 — Submit
  Fork the repository → add your entry → open a Pull Request.
```

Contributions are reviewed for depth and originality — not for whether the reviewer agrees with your conclusion.

---

## / Contribution Standards

```
  [+]  Original analysis and perspective
  [+]  Engagement with the source's actual argument
  [+]  Honest uncertainty — say what you don't know
  [+]  Specific claims over vague generalities
  [+]  Respectful but direct disagreement

  [-]  Summaries without interpretation
  [-]  Agreement without interrogation
  [-]  Personal attacks or dismissiveness
  [-]  Speed over substance
```

> **Depth is the only metric that matters here.**
